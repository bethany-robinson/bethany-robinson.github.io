Knitting

Blocking is Magic

One of my favorite design elements to knit is lace. There's something very satisfying and programmatic about Following a chart, which then translates into a beautiful fabric. When the lace pattern first is knit, the pattern can be difficult to see because the stitches tend to be tight. When the fabric is stretched, it's easier to see glimpses of the full pattern, but the fabric doesn't stay in that position. To get the fabric to open up and for the lace to be visible, a technique called Blocking is used.

Blocking knitting consists of soaking the completed project in water with some wool wash and then pinning the piece in the shape you'd like it to dry. In the case of lace, this can involve the use of stiff wires and pins to stretch the lace into the desired shape. Wool and other natural fibers will dry in the shape they are placed. Unblocked Shawl
